QUESTION & ANSWER

Q: What's your favorite documentary?

A: My favorite documentary of all time is probably the film The King of Kong. The documentary follows two pro Donkey Kong players, Billy Mitchell and Steve Wiebe, in their quest for domination over the game's high score. It's such a ridiculous concept but the people involved really treat it as a way of life and the documentary captures these people so well. Billy Mitchell makes a great villain.

Q: What kind of work do you enjoy?

A: I like working on video editing projects, particularly on documentaries or real-life stories. Part of the fun is going and experiencing whatever you might be documenting. For instance, I followed my friends who rock climb for 16 hours and got footage of them competing in a competition. It was fun to create a video around that, but the actual work of following that story was 75% of the job!

Q: What do you like to do in your free time?

A: I like to read, hike, work out, and spend time with friends typically in my free time. I used to be more of a video game player, but they don't tend to hold my interest for super long anymore. If I do pick up a game it'll be Pokemon - I love me some Pokemon. Always have. I also love to watch the reality TV show Survivor. Definitely my guilty pleasure, but I'm not that guilty about it since I genuinely think it's a great TV show!

Q: What's your favorite snack?

A: I really enjoy the Jalepeno & Cream Cheese Wontons you can get from Target. I'm talking about the name brand Good & Gather wontons. Sounds like a paid advertisement but sadly Target is not paying me to say this. In fact, I'm in the hole about 8 dollars more every time I buy them. It's worth it, though. They're really good. I enjoy them.

Q: What's your favorite book?

A: As of recently, my favorite book I've read is "Into Thin Air" by Jon Krakauer. It documents the horrifying disaster on Mount Everest in 1996, which Krakauer was on the expedition for. It was a deeply profound and moving book, and really put the dangers of mountaineering into perspective. It made me think more deeply about mortality than usual - very touching and impactful story as well as a really well-written account of the events that unfolded.

That's all for now! Thanks!
