LIFE & DEATH IN THE MOUNTAINS

My midterm project was focused on death in nature, particularly among plant life. I emphasized beige, red, and overall more dull hues to convey the emotion behind my theme. For my final project, there were a couple of things I wanted to build on from my previous set of photos. I wanted to focus on a theme that would provide a contrast to the previous one, so I focused on budding plant life around Colorado - “life in the mountains” as opposed to “death in the desert”. Initially I thought I would stay with the same thing, but the time of year is just perfect for life and there were so many little gems blooming around Boulder.

Another thing I wanted to expand upon from my previous project was close-up shots: I really, really enjoyed how my more intimate shots from Moab and the Grand Canyon turned out and wanted to make more of an effort to take close-up shots with more shallow depth of field. Since the signs of life are still smaller right now, and it took effort to scout out tiny small flowers, this style of photography fit the project perfectly.

In general I wanted to highlight the diversity of plant life in the mountains and I think I did a good job of doing so. The amount of colors, and small things you can find in nature is just fantastic and I wanted this project to serve as inspiration to pay attention to the smaller things in nature. Often we pay attention to the big picture when in the wilderness, and while that’s also quite beautiful I find it rewarding to seek out the pieces of the natural world which usually evade us. Looking for those small sprouts of life was a challenge, and some of them ended up being beautiful compositions which provided a good dichotomy with my midterm while still being thematically related.
